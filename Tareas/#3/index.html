Guitar Calibration

What's that?

Someone has happened to your guitar sounds bad, out of tune or detune during you play, note played is buzzing or muted, play became uncomfortable for your hands, this means the guitar is out of calibration. The Guitar Calibration is the best way to fix that problem.

See More info. For some examples and threads look this forum.

How does it work?

When you have a uncalibrated guitar first is necessary find a potential problems. The most common problems are the strings detune after you tuned them, guitar pickups screech, has a low gain or in the worse case the pickup is dead. Other common problem is the neck, when this are concave or convex. Now the calibrating come to fix this common problems in the guitar, to get the maximum quality and most important, greater comfort and reliability. So here you can find and learn a solution for you uncalibrated guitar, the use or application of the techniques shown in the site becomes the responsibility of whoever use that.

If you can't find a solution, please tell us. Contact at the end of the page.

Tools nedeed.

Here mention which tool need you, beginning to the more important and finally with the optional tools.

1. Screwdriver:

This is the most important tool because in the process you could find many cross screws and flat screws. For a screwdriver the size goes by number. You'll need these.

      Phillip Screwdriver #2
      Phillip Screwdriver #3
      Phillip Screwdriver #4
      Flat Screwdriver #4
      Phillip Screwdriver #1 (Optional)
      Flat Screwdriver #2 (Optional)

2. Needle-nose pliers (can cut wires):

With this tool you can grab, hold, pull and cut some pieces including wires and the guitar strings.

3. Ruler:

The ruler need begin in 0 mm and size be over 6 inches.

4. Hex Key Set or/and Truss Rod Wrench:

Is important have a hex key set because some parts in the guitar used this kind of key. In case of the truss rod wrench first look your truss rod type, if your guitar has this kind of truss rod is fundamental buy the truss rod wrench, this in case if you don't have wrench because in most cases the guitar come with the truss rod wrench.

5. Adjustable spanner or wrench set:

Any two option works.

6. Oil for sewing machine or any lubricant for a metal parts:

This is important because sometime parts don't out and is good to lubricate the new guitar strings.

7. Soldering, solder wire and soldering holder (Optional):

Maybe you can need this tools, but only if your guitar has a circuit problem or need fix some wire connection.

Preparation in advance.

Before you get started, check this out.

Is important have a comfortable work spot enough to fit the guitar and tools. Put a soft thing over the work area, this for don't damage guitar.

Find the common problems

Look what kind of problem your guitar has and the solution.

Toggle selector or knobs don't work? You need check and fix them or replace damaged part. Skip to solution

Your guitar detuned fast when you play? Head Tuners are failing or your guitar is uncalibrated. Skip to solution

Pickups lose gain or volume when you play? Potentiometers are failing, Is necessary adjust the pickups height or probably is a connection problem.

The guitar sound works good but buzzing after you plug a sound cable? Your guitar has a problem with ground connection.

Guitar emits noise when you playing, as screams or buzzing sound? Some wire don't have connection, the pickups are failing or the jack plug doesn't good.

The strings height is very high or low? You need adjust the bridge height.

Note played is buzzing or muted, play became uncomfortable for your hands? Full calibration...

Carefully, applied force and moves the knob slowly, until this can spin again. You can use a Needle-nose pliers but be carefully.

Is time to clean! use a little piece of sandpaper, roll this and insert in to the jack output, move this inside until you clean all cavity.

If the problem continues, change a new jack.

Tuning problems.

    1. Head tuners are failing.

Use a brush to clean all tuners, put oil or metal lubricant in the tuner and spin this. Check if all screws exist. In the worse case replace the piece.
